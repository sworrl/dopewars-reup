# Anti-glorification policy

This game uses the realism of drug-trade simulation as an *educational* lever, not a romanticization of drug use or sale. Tone is *The Wire* / Cormac McCarthy, not *Grand Theft Auto*.

## The rule

When evaluating any feature, ask:

> Does this make a player more likely to romanticize drug use or trade?

If yes → redesign or cut.

## What this looks like in practice

### Yes

- **Boot-screen reveal** — first launch geolocates (with permission) and shows the player their actual local area's overdose deaths/year, top three substances, arrest stats, child welfare cases, demographics. *Then* character creation. "This is your block."
- **Visible overdose events** — NPC overdoses tied to player's local market activity. Cutting product (especially with fentanyl) sharpens OD spike rates in the news feed.
- **Family / community impact** — NPCs have visible families and jobs that degrade as the local drug scene worsens. Player busts make in-game local news.
- **Exit ramps as real gameplay paths** — informant career, recovery clinic owner endgame, run for local office on a reform platform, launder into legal cannabis where applicable.
- **Real federal data in the world** — CDC WONDER, DEA NDTA, FBI UCR/NIBRS, state health dashboards. Cited in the About screen.
- **Real federal agencies kept real** — DEA, FBI, ATF, USPS Inspection. Players learn actual law-enforcement structure.

### No

- No "fun" use animations. Drug *use* is depicted clinically — lost customers, OD lawsuits, addict snitches as business problems.
- No "show off your drug life" cosmetics. Supporter cosmetics lean into criminal aesthetic (a tailored 70s suit) but not drug-use aesthetic (no "rolling fat blunt" emote).
- No leaderboard for "kills" or "ODs caused" — the community-harm metrics are visible but not gamified for prestige.
- No "first dose feels great" framing for in-game addicts. Addiction NPCs show consequences first.

- [NEXT Distro](https://nextdistro.org/) — naloxone and syringe access by mail.
