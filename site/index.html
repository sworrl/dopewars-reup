The world keeps moving when you close the app. Your crew keeps pushing. Your phone keeps draining. Rivals keep moving.

You are a small operator in a real American city, rendered from actual OpenStreetMap data. Prices move by region and demand. Product has weight and takes up space. Police run on dice and heat, not scripted cutscenes.

It is modeled on real reporting: DEA and NIDA pricing, FBI and CDC regional stats. It is framed to educate, not glamorize. Nobody is shown using.

// systems online

Everything is a decision

Geography

Real map

Actual OSM streets and OSRM routes. Trips cost real distance times mode speed. Stores sit at real coordinates under parody names.

Economy

Live market

Real baseline prices drift by region and demand. The server sets every price, so it cannot be forged. Product art changes with weight.

Logistics

Weight is real

Carry it, sell it down, or delegate a stash to a trap-house crew that pushes it while you sleep, for a skim and some heat.

Gear

The phone

Burner is anonymous but dumb. Flagship is capable but tracked. Hardened OS keeps you dark. A cracked screen degrades the display.

Prestige

Cred, not cash

An earned currency for 100 cosmetic items. Emblems, titles, nameplates, badges. Never bought with real money, never an advantage.

Online

Unseen rivals

Opted-in players share the map. You notice each other only through observed crime or a perception check. Whispers can be overheard.
